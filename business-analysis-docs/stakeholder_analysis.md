# 👥 Stakeholder Analysis
### Project: Employee Attrition Analytics System

---

## 🔍 What is a Stakeholder?
A stakeholder is **anyone who is affected by or interested in this project**. They don't need to build it — they just need to care about it.

Example: If you build an attrition dashboard, the HR Head doesn't write code — but they USE the dashboard every day. So they are a stakeholder.

## 2. 📋 Stakeholder Details

### 👤 Stakeholder 1 — CEO
| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Role** | Company Sponsor |
| **Why they care** | Attrition costs the company money — they want it reduced |
| **What they want from this project** | A clear number: "How much are we losing and what should we do?" |
| **How often they see the dashboard** | Monthly (executive summary only) |
| **Power / Influence** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High |
| **Interest in details** | Low — they want summary, not deep analysis |

## 3. 📊 Stakeholder Matrix (Power vs Interest)

```
High Power  |  CEO              |  HR Head
            |  (Keep Satisfied) |  (Manage Closely)
            |------------------------------------
Low Power   |  (Monitor)        |  HR Managers
            |                   |  Data Analyst
            |                   |  (Keep Informed)
            |---------------------|----------------
                Low Interest        High Interest
```

### What this means:
| Quadrant | Who | What to do |
|----------|-----|------------|
| High Power + High Interest | HR Head | Keep them most updated |
| High Power + Low Interest | CEO | Give them only summary reports |
| Low Power + High Interest | HR Managers, Data Analyst | Keep them informed regularly |
